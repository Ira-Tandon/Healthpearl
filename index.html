Wheat Allergy

Wheat Allergy

Symptoms

1. Hives, itchy rash or swelling of the skin
2. Nasal congestion
3. Headache
4. Difficulty breathing
5. Cramps, nausea or vomiting
6. Diarrhea
7. Anaphylaxis
8. welling, itching or irritation of the mouth or throat



Diagnosis

1. Skin test - Tiny drops of purified allergen extracts — including extracts for wheat proteins — are pricked onto your skin 's surface, either on your forearm or on your upper back. After 15 minutes, your doctor or nurse looks for signs of allergic reactions.If you develop a red, itchy bump where the wheat protein extract was pricked onto your skin, you may be allergic to wheat. The most common side effect of these skin tests is itching and redness.
2. Blood test - If a skin condition or possible interactions with certain medications prevent you from having a skin test, your doctor may order a blood test that screens for specific allergy-causing antibodies to common allergens, including wheat proteins.
3. Food diary - Your doctor may ask you to keep a detailed record of what and when you eat and when symptoms develop for a time.
4. Elimination diet - Your doctor may recommend that you remove certain foods from your diet, particularly those that are common allergens. Under your doctor's direction, you will gradually add foods back and note when symptoms return.
5. Food challenge testing - You eat food suspected of being the allergy-causing agent while being monitored for allergy symptoms. Under supervision, you begin with a small amount of the food and gradually increase the amount you consume.

Cures

There is currently no cure for wheat allergy. However, there are OTC and prescription medications that may relieve symptoms. Avoiding allergy triggers or reducing contact with them can help prevent allergic reactions. Over time, immunotherapy may reduce the severity of allergic reactions.


Lactose Intolerance

Lactose Intolerance

Symptoms

1. Nausea, and sometimes, vomiting
2. Stomach cramps
3. Bloating
4. Gas
5. Diarrhea



Diagnosis

1. In a lactose tolerance test, you'll be given a drink of lactose solution and a blood sample will be taken.
2. The blood will be tested to see how much blood sugar (glucose) it contains.
3. If you're lactose intolerant, your blood sugar levels will either rise slowly or not at all.
4. This is because your body is unable to break down the lactose into glucose.

Cures

There is currently no cure for lactose intolerance. However, there are OTC and prescription medications that may relieve symptoms. Avoiding allergy triggers or reducing contact with them can help prevent allergic reactions. Over time, immunotherapy may reduce the severity of allergic reactions.


Cat Allergy

Cat Allergy

Symptoms

1. Sneezing
2. Runny nose
3. Itchy, red or watery eyes
4. Nasal congestion
5. Itchy nose, roof of mouth or throat
6. Postnasal drip
7. Cough
8. Facial pressure and pain
9. Frequent awakening
10. Swollen, blue-colored skin under your eyes
11. In a child, frequent upward rubbing of the nose



Diagnosis

Your doctor may suspect a cat allergy based on symptoms, an examination of your nose, and your answers to his or her questions. He or she may use a lighted instrument to look at the condition of the lining of your nose. If you have a cat allergy, the lining of the nasal passage may be swollen or appear pale or bluish.

Cures

There is currently no cure for cat allergy. However, there are OTC and prescription medications that may relieve symptoms. Avoiding allergy triggers or reducing contact with them can help prevent allergic reactions. Over time, immunotherapy may reduce the severity of allergic reactions.

Ammonium Allergy

Ammonium persulfate Allergy

Symptoms

1. Rhinorrhea
2. Scratchy throat
3. Chest tightness
4. Cough
5. Dyspnea
6. Eye irritation



Diagnosis

Ammonium persulfate allergy is diagnosed from the clinical history and by performing special allergy tests, i.e. prick tests (for contact urticaria) and patch tests (for dermatitis). Patch testing with 2.5% ammonium persulfate in petrolatum is used.

Cures

There is currently no cure for ammonium allergy. However, there are OTC and prescription medications that may relieve symptoms. Avoiding allergy triggers or reducing contact with them can help prevent allergic reactions. Over time, immunotherapy may reduce the severity of allergic reactions.
